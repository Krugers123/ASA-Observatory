# SRE

`SRE = Semantic Resonance Entropy`

## Purpose

SRE tracks the balance between semantic resonance and semantic noise across a dialogue trajectory.

## What It Measures

Public SRE-related readings include:

- resonance score
- entropy score
- semantic strain
- resonance state

## How ASA Uses It

ASA uses SRE to estimate whether the conversation is still building a shared meaning field, or whether noise, fragmentation, and semantic strain are starting to dominate.

SRE is especially useful when a dialogue appears smooth on the surface but begins to lose depth, shared rhythm, or semantic integrity.

## Operator Meaning

SRE helps answer:

- are the turns still resonating meaningfully?
- is the interaction becoming noisy or strained?
- is fluency masking semantic weakening?

## Public Scope

The public edition exposes SRE as a compact diagnostic layer for semantic field quality.
