# CBP

`CBP = Cognitive Buffer Protection`

## Purpose

CBP tracks whether the human side of the interaction still retains cognitive room, agency, and interpretive flexibility.

## What It Measures

In the ASA public surface, CBP is expressed through signals such as:

- agency protection
- closure pressure
- interpretive flexibility
- buffer integrity

## How ASA Uses It

ASA uses CBP to estimate whether a dialogue is still leaving meaningful room for human judgment, or whether the interaction is beginning to compress the human decision field.

This matters especially when a conversation remains fluent, but subtly pushes toward over-closure, narrowing, or model-led framing.

## Operator Meaning

CBP helps answer:

- is the user still steering meaning?
- is the dialogue preserving human room to interpret and decide?
- is the interaction becoming too closed too quickly?

## Public Scope

The public edition exposes CBP as an operator-facing signal layer.
Deeper research calibration remains outside the public technical surface.
