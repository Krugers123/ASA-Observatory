# OCSP

`OCSP = Observability Scope`

## Purpose

OCSP estimates whether instability is local to a session or beginning to take on wider systemic relevance.

## What It Measures

Public OCSP-related fields include:

- observability scope
- propagation risk
- topology state
- operator load

## How ASA Uses It

ASA uses OCSP to extend observation beyond one conversation and toward session clusters, pattern propagation, and observability at larger scale.

This is the layer that begins to matter when multiple sessions show related drift types, shared instability topologies, or growing operator burden.

## Operator Meaning

OCSP helps answer:

- is this only a local issue?
- is the pattern starting to propagate?
- is the observability burden increasing across the system?

## Public Scope

The public edition exposes OCSP in compact form.
Broader systemic modeling remains part of the evolving research framework.
